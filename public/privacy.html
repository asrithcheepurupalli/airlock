Privacy policy

Effective 27 June 2026

Airlock is built so that this page can be short. It processes everything on your device. It collects nothing, sends nothing, and stores nothing on any server.

The whole thing in one line Airlock has no servers, no analytics, and no network permission. Your data never reaches us, because there is no "us" for it to reach.

What Airlock does

Airlock detects sensitive content in your prompt (emails, cards, secrets, identifiers, and names, organisations and places via a small model that runs in your browser), replaces each value with a placeholder, and lets you send only the placeholder version to an AI assistant. When the reply returns, Airlock restores your real values locally so it reads naturally. The key map that links placeholders to real values lives only in your browser and is never transmitted.

What we collect

Nothing. Airlock has no accounts, no analytics, no telemetry, and no tracking. It makes no network requests of its own. You can confirm this: open your browser's network tab and watch Airlock stay silent.

Permissions, and why

  • storage saves your settings and your own sensitive-term list locally in your browser. It is never synced to us.
  • offscreen runs the on-device name-detection model in a background page on your machine.
  • Airlock requests no network or host permission of its own. It only adds a redaction layer to the AI sites you already use.

Your data and third parties

We do not sell, share, or transmit your data, because we never receive it. Airlock has no third-party services. When you choose to send a redacted prompt to ChatGPT, Claude or Gemini, that text goes to those providers under their own policies. Airlock's entire job is to make sure the sensitive parts are replaced with placeholders before that happens.

Children

Airlock is a professional tool and is not directed to children under 13.

If this policy changes, we will update the date above and post the new version here.
